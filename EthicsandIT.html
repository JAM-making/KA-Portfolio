Ethics and IT: IT Equipment Usage and Abuse Prevention Guidelines and Policies

Week 5 Compilation | Anna Kephart | ITS1103 Ethics & Information Technology | August 12, 2024

Purpose Statement

These guidelines establish a framework for responsible and secure use of IT resources at Ajax Digital Information (ADI). They protect company assets, prevent cyberattacks, and ensure employee compliance with legal and ethical standards.

IT Equipment Usage and Abuse Prevention Guidelines

Addressing Denial of Service

  1. Network Usage Awareness: Monitor bandwidth, get approval for large transfers, submit hardware requests properly.
  2. Software Usage: Only install approved software; personal file storage is prohibited.
  3. Email Management: Maintain organized email accounts and prevent system shutdowns.

Mitigating Cyberattacks and Cybersecurity

Prevention: Participate in annual cybersecurity training, phishing awareness, social engineering training; use strong passwords; enable MFA; keep systems updated; maintain firewalls and antivirus software.

Detection: Report any suspicious activity immediately to IT.

Remediation: Report unauthorized software, hardware, or data usage to IT.

Ethical Decisions in Software Development

  • Respect user privacy and avoid unethical data collection.
  • Obtain informed consent for data collection, including third-party vendors.
  • Deliver thoroughly tested, high-quality software that meets customer needs.

Comprehensive Data Privacy and IT Usage Policy

  • Data Privacy Policy: Confidentiality, integrity, and availability of data; secure storage and restricted access; immediate breach reporting.
  • Corporate IT Usage Policy: Conserve network resources, comply with legal requirements, minimize non-business usage.
  • Internet Access: Restricted non-business websites; anonymous remailers prohibited.
  • Content Restrictions: Defamation, hate speech, and pornography prohibited; monitored regularly.
  • Reporting and Compliance: Anonymous complaint handling; monitored via logs and security systems.

Internet and Email Usage Policy

  • Use Internet primarily for business; avoid security-risk websites.
  • Use email primarily for business; avoid inappropriate content.
  • Use company-approved antivirus; report malware immediately.

Social Media Code of Conduct

  • Include disclaimers like “opinions are my own”; identify as ADI employee.
  • Maintain confidentiality of company data and product releases.
  • Post diplomatically about competitors.
  • Respond to complaints respectfully; protect privacy; disciplinary actions for violations.

Contingent Worker Policy

  • Clearly define contingent workers; finite assignments; legal compliance.
  • Use worker’s own equipment; avoid direct supervision or training.
  • Ensure leasing firms provide benefits and comply with regulations.

Intellectual Property – Copyright

  • Use only licensed software; comply with EULAs.
  • Use approved shareware; avoid malware; get cybersecurity approval.
  • Follow software piracy rules; report violations.

Privacy in the Workplace

  • Workplace monitoring applies to emails, internet logs, chats, and phones; personal belongings remain private unless part of investigation.
  • Comply with HIPAA for health records.
  • Surveillance limited to common areas; GDPR compliance for personal data.
  • Violations may result in disciplinary actions; appeal process is available.
